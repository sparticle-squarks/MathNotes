Rational Numbers, Non-Terminating Decimals, and Percents Fractions with Negative Exponents

Rational can be expressed as a ratio of two integers, for example, `1/8=0.125`

Expressing a ratio of two integers can result in terminating (rational) or non terminating decimal (irrational) like `1/11= 0.09090909... or 0.bar(09)`

`class{cmjx-note}{"Note : When using irrational numbers in computations, either they should be expressed as equivalent fraction or round the decimal to an appropriate number of decimal places"}`

Squences

Arthmetic sequence: Differnece between any two consequtive terms is constant. The constant difference is called common difference d.

Derivation of nth term of arithmetic sequnce

`a_2 = a_1+d class{cmjx-term_definition}{ "Where: "a_1" - first term, d - common difference"`
`a_3 = a_2+d`
`a_4 = a_3+d`
`...`
`a_n = a_(n-1)+d`
`class{cmjx-note}{"Note: the three dots '...' are called ellipsis"}`

`:.`
`a_2 = a_1+d`
`a_3 = (a_1+d)+d`
`a_4 = ((a_1+d)+d)+d`
`...`
`a_n = a_1+(n-2)d+d` `=> class{cmjx-redbox}{a_n = a_1+(n-1)d}`

Geometric sequence: There of any two consequtive terms is constant. The constant ratio is called common ratio and denoted by r.

Derivation of nth term of geometric sequnce

`a_2 = a_1r class{cmjx-term_definition}{ "Where: "a_1" - first term, r - common ratio"`
`a_3 = a_2r`
`a_4 = a_3r`
`...`
`a_n = a_(n-1)r`

`:.`
`a_2 = a_1r`
`a_3 = (a_1r)r`
`a_4 = ((a_1r)r)r`
`...`
`a_n = a_1r^(n-2)r` `=> class{cmjx-redbox}{a_n = a_1r^(n-1)}`

A percent of a Whole is a Part

`class{cmjx-redbox}{% xx W=P}`

Example

`"32 ounces is 25% of a gallon. How many ounces in a gallon?"`
`"using " % xx W = P`
`25% xx W = 32 oz`
`=> W = (32 oz)/(25%)`
`=>W=32/(25/100)oz`
`=> W = 32*100/25oz`
`=> W = 128oz`

Area of the trapezoid

Definition

A trapezoid is a four-sided closed 2D figure which has an area and its perimeter. Two sides of the shape are parallel to each other and they are termed as the bases of the trapezoid. The non-parallel sides are known as the legs or lateral sides of a trapezoid. The shortest distance between two parallel sides is known as the altitude.

Monomial, binomial, trinomial and polynomial

Monomial

A monomial consists of one term and is a constant or the product of constants and/or variables with exponents that are whole numbers.
Examples
`2x^3", "-5", "x^2y", "7xyz", "z`

Binomial

A binomial consists of two terms.
Examples
`x+5", "xy-7y", "x^2-4`

Trinomial

A trinomial consists of three terms.
Examples
`x^2+7x+10", "x+2xy+y`

Monomials, binomials, and trinomials are all types of polynomials and can have any number of terms.

The degree of a polynomial is the highest degree of any of its term.
If a term has one variable, the degree of the term is the exponent of the variable.
`{: (" ",y,"exponent is 1, first degree term","(linear)",), (" ",b^2,"exponent is 2, second degree term","(quadratic)",), (" ",p^3,"exponent is 3, third degree term","(cubic)",) :}`
