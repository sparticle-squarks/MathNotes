Squences

Arthmetic sequence: Differnece between any two consequtive terms is constant. The constant difference is called common difference d.

Derivation of nth term of arithmetic sequnce

`a_2 = a_1+d class{cmjx-term_definition}{ "Where: "a_1" - first term, d - common difference"`
`a_3 = a_2+d`
`a_4 = a_3+d`
`...`
`a_n = a_(n-1)+d`
`class{cmjx-note}{"Note: the three dots '...' are called ellipsis"}`

`:.`
`a_2 = a_1+d`
`a_3 = (a_1+d)+d`
`a_4 = ((a_1+d)+d)+d`
`...`
`a_n = a_1+(n-2)d+d` `=> class{cmjx-redbox}{a_n = a_1+(n-1)d}`

Geometric sequence: There of any two consequtive terms is constant. The constant ratio is called common ratio and denoted by r.

Derivation of nth term of geometric sequnce

`a_2 = a_1r class{cmjx-term_definition}{ "Where: "a_1" - first term, r - common ratio"`
`a_3 = a_2r`
`a_4 = a_3r`
`...`
`a_n = a_(n-1)r`

`:.`
`a_2 = a_1r`
`a_3 = (a_1r)r`
`a_4 = ((a_1r)r)r`
`...`
`a_n = a_1r^(n-2)r` `=> class{cmjx-redbox}{a_n = a_1r^(n-1)}`

Solving simultaneous linear equations

Say there are following two independant equations,
`Ax+By=C class{cmjx-equation_number}{(1)}` `Cx+Dy=E class{cmjx-equation_number}{(2)}`
A solution that satisfies both equations simultaneously can be found by finding the point of intersection between two lines formed by two linear equations `(1) and (2)`

Proof :

From previous section we know that a linear equation when plotted, will be a straight line and every point on the line is a solution for the linear equation.
`:. " points on line formed by "Ax+By=C" are solutions for that linear equation"`
similarly points on line formed by "Cx+Dy=E are solutions for the equation"
`:. " a point where both lines intersect lines on both equations. Hence the point of intersection is solution for both equations"`

Exponential Function:
An exponential function is the product of an initial value and constant ratio raised to a power.

`f(x) = a * b^x`

where a is the initial value and b is the constant ratio and `a != 0 and b > 0`.
